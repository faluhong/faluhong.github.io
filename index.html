Research & Publications (Google Scholar) (Researchgate)

Evaluations of diurnal land surface temperature cycle (DTC) models under clear-sky
  • Obtain nine representative four-parameter DTC models with a set of parameter-reduction strategies.
  • Comprehensive assessment of DTC models with geostationary satellite data and in-situ measurements.
  • Provide the parameter-reduction order and the best-performance four parameter DTC model.
[1] Hong, F.,., Zhan, W., Göttsche, F.M., Liu, Z., Zhou, J., Huang, F., Lai, J., Li, M. (2018). Comprehensive assessment of four-parameter diurnal land surface temperature cycle models under clear-sky. ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing, 142, 190-204. (Link) (PDF)
Design of a framework to estimate accurate daily mean land surface temperature from tandem polar-orbiting satellites
  • Design a framework which combines annual temperature cycle and diurnal temperature cycle models to estimate accurate daily mean land surface temperature.
  • Evaluate the accuracy and sensitivity of the framework with geostationary satellite data under clear-sky condition and in-situ measurements under cloudy condition.
  • Quantify the sampling bias caused by simply averaging sporadic and instantaneous clear-sky observations.
[1] Hong, F., Zhan, W. et al., A simple yet robust framework to estimate accurate daily mean land surface temperature from thermal observations of tandem polar orbiters. (Under review)
Generation of a 17-year spatiotemporally seamless dataset of global daily mean land surface temperature
  • Generate global all-weather daily mean land surface temperature products by combining remote sensing data from polar-orbiting satellites and reanalysis data.
  • Validate the generated products with widely-distributed in-situ measurements.
  • Analyze the global LST trend from 2003 to 2019.
[1] Hong, F., Zhan, W. et al., A 17-year spatiotemporally seamless global dataset of daily mean land surface temperature: generation, validation, and analysis. (in preparation)
