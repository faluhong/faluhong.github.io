Short bio: Falu Hong is now a master student in the final year project at Nanjing University. Since 2016, Falu has been working with Prof. Wenfeng Zhan and majors in the theory and application of thermal remote sensing. He specially focuses on improving and enhancing the diurnal and annual land surface temperature cycle models. At the same time, he applies these temporal interpolation models to analyze the trending of land surface temperature. In his free time, Falu enjoys playing tennis and swimming.

Research & Publications (Google Scholar) (Researchgate)

Evaluations of diurnal land surface temperature cycle (DTC) models under clear-sky (supervised independent research)
  • Obtain nine representative four-parameter DTC models with a set of parameter-reduction strategies.
  • Comprehensive assessment of DTC models with geostationary satellite data and in-situ measurements.
  • Provide the parameter-reduction order and the best-performance four parameter DTC model.
[1] Hong, F.,., Zhan, W., Göttsche, F.M., Liu, Z., Zhou, J., Huang, F., Lai, J., Li, M. (2018). Comprehensive assessment of four-parameter diurnal land surface temperature cycle models under clear-sky. ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing, 142, 190-204. (Link) (PDF)
Designing a framework to generate physically true daily mean land surface temperature from thermal remote sensing satellite data (supervised independent research, ongoing)
  • Design a framework which combines annual temperature cycle and diurnal temperature cycle models to generate physically true daily mean land surface temperature.
  • Evaluate the accuracy and sensitivity of the framework with geostationary satellite data under clear-sky condition and in-situ measurements under cloudy condition.
  • Quantify the sampling bias caused by simply averaging sporadic clear-sky observations.
[1] Hong, F., Zhan, W. et al., Fast generation of physically true daily land surface temperatures using thermal observations from tandem polar-orbiters, Part I: Framework and validation, in preparation. (Target Journal: Remote Sensing of Environment)
Fast generation of global daily mean land surface temperature products based on satellite data (supervised independent research, ongoing)
  • Generate global all-weather daily mean 1-km land surface temperature products by combining remote sensing data from polar-orbiters and reanalysis data.
  • Employ downscaling technique to boost the calculation speed based on the new designed framework.
  • Analyze the global temporal trend of LST variations from 2002 to 2017.
[1] Hong, F., Zhan, W. et al., Fast generation of physically true daily land surface temperatures using thermal observations from tandem polar-orbiters, Part II: Application to global scale and sampling bias analysis, in preparation. (Target Journal: Remote Sensing of Environment)
Enhancement of the annual land surface temperature cycle (ATC) model (cooperation, core member)
  • Enhance the traditional sinusoidal ATC model by consideration of short-term weather fluctuations with the assistance of LST-related controlling factors.
  • Propose a hybrid and flexible framework to balance the prediction accuracy and generalization ability of ATC models.
[1] Zou, Z., Zhan, W., Liu, Z., Bechtel, B., Gao, L., Hong, F., Huang, F., Lai, J. (2018). Enhanced modeling of annual temperature cycles with temporally discrete remotely sensed thermal observations. Remote Sensing, 10(4), 650. (Link) (PDF)
[2] Liu, Z., Zhan, W., Lai, J., Hong, F., Quan, J., Bechtel, B., Huang, F., Zou, Z. (2019). Balancing prediction accuracy and generalization ability: A hybrid framework for modelling the annual dynamics of satellite-derived land surface temperatures. ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing, 151, 189-206. (Link) (PDF)
Identification of typical diurnal patterns of surface urban heat islands (cooperation)
  • Apply the best four-parameter diurnal temperature cycle model to depict the diurnal SUHI.
  • Identify five typical diurnal SUHI temporal patterns from 354 Chinese megacities.
